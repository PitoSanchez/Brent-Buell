THEATER & TRANSFORMATION

Brent Buell is a playwright, producer, director, and novelist. He co-produced A24’s movie Sing Sing. For ten years, Buell directed theater with Rehabilitation Through the Arts (RTA) in New York’s maximum-security prisons, working on plays ranging from Of Mice and Men to original works by prisoners. His work has been praised by critics, including The New York Times. His play Breakin’ the Mummy’s Code, premiered inside Sing Sing and inspired A24’s Sing Sing.

CREATIVE IMPACT

Buell’s contributions extend to creating impactful theater, such as The Gem Exchange, Wood Bars, and the Las Vegas spectacle Undone Divas. He has directed numerous works, including unFRAMED by Iyaba Ibo Mandingo, From Sing Sing to Broadway, and The Gospel According to Josh. His directorial work is recognized for its ability to transcend boundaries and create meaningful narratives.

STORYTELLING LEGACY

Buell’s storytelling continues through his novels, including the political parody Rapturous and Daniel and My Revelation. His memoir, Sing Sing Backstage, documenting his years directing theater in prison, is set to be published. Buell's career showcases the transformative power of theater, with evidence showing less than 3% of RTA members returning to prison, compared to over 60% nationally.
