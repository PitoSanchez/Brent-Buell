About Brent Buell

Welcome to the world of Brent Buell, a multi-talented creative force whose work spans theater, film, and literature. Brent is a playwright, producer, director, and novelist whose passion for storytelling knows no bounds.

For over ten years, Brent Buell brought powerful stories to life inside the walls of New York's maximum-security prisons, working with Rehabilitation Through the Arts (RTA). His directorial work included productions of classics like John Steinbeck's Of Mice and Men, as well as original pieces written by incarcerated individuals. His plays were met with acclaim, even drawing praise from The New York Times. His comedy Breakin' the Mummy's Code, which premiered inside Sing Sing, was highlighted in an Esquire article by bestselling author John Richardson and became an inspiration for the A24 film Sing Sing. In that film, Brent himself is portrayed by Oscar-nominated actor Paul Raci.

Brent's belief in the transformative power of theater is evident through the impact of RTA: less than 3% of RTA participants ever return to prison, compared to the national average of over 60%. This work, and his chapter titled “Drama in the Big House” in Jonathan Shailor’s Performing New Lives, Prison Theater, documents these transformative experiences.

Brent's original works include The Gem Exchange, a comedy; Wood Bars, written with Miguel Valentin for the opening of Lin-Manuel Miranda, John Buffalo Mailer, and Tom Kail’s Back House Productions; and The Gate, a drama about parole co-written with Charles Moore. He also collaborated on the Las Vegas spectacular Undone Divas with Madelene Capelle. Brent has directed a variety of works including unFRAMED by Iyaba Ibo Mandingo, From Sing Sing to Broadway, and Deathbed by Mark Schultz, among others.

In addition to his theater credits, Brent has taken part in several film projects. He wrote and directed The Terrors of Teri, a film for Ohio University, and directed Moses, an award-winning comedy for Goddess Films. He has also appeared in films like Grand Opening and Al Quarem.

His foray into the world of literature includes the novels Rapturous, a political parody, and Daniel and My Revelation. Brent is currently working on his memoir, Sing Sing Backstage, which chronicles his years of directing in prison and is set to be published in the near future.

Brent Buell's work has always been about creating art that not only entertains but also inspires change and challenges our perspectives on justice and humanity. Welcome to his world, where storytelling breaks barriers and redefines what it means to be human.
